The graph of the solution set of a linear inequality is a half-plane, and the graph may include the boundary line. This means every point in one half-plane represents a solution to the inequality, and there are no solutions represented by points in the other half-plane. Either all of the ordered pairs of the points on the boundary line are solutions to the inequality or they are not solutions to the inequality.
